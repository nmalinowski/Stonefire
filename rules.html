How to Play Stonefire

Stonefire is a turn-based trading card game set in a prehistoric world. Build a deck from powerful dinosaurs and ancient creatures, then battle against the AI opponent to reduce their hero to 0 health.

Game Basics

Setup

  • Each player starts with 30 health
  • Each player has a deck of 50 cards
  • Draw 4 cards at the start of the game
  • The board has 7 slots for creatures on each side

Turn Structure

  1. Start of Turn: Gain +1 maximum mana (up to 10), refill your mana, draw a card
  2. Main Phase: Play cards from your hand and attack with your creatures
  3. End Turn: Click the "End Turn" button to pass to your opponent

Victory Condition

Reduce your opponent's hero to 0 health to win the game. If a player has no cards left to draw and their hand is empty, they lose.

Combat

Attacking

  1. Click on one of your creatures that can attack (shown with a green glow)
  2. Click on an enemy creature or the enemy hero
  3. Both creatures deal their Attack damage to each other simultaneously
  4. Creatures reduced to 0 or less Health are destroyed

Combat Rules

  • Summoning Sickness: Creatures cannot attack the turn they're played (unless they have Charge)
  • Guard Priority: If the enemy has a creature with Guard, you must attack it first
  • Simultaneous Damage: Both creatures in combat deal damage at the same time
  • Armored Reduction: Armored creatures take X less damage from attacks
  • Venomous Kills: Any damage from a Venomous creature instantly kills the target creature

Strategy Tips

Mana Efficiency

Try to use all your mana each turn. Playing a 2-cost and 3-cost card on turn 5 is better than just playing one 4-cost card and wasting 1 mana.

Board Control

Trading your creatures into enemy creatures to clear the board is often better than attacking the enemy hero directly. A creature left alive will deal damage every turn!

Guard Usage

Place Guard creatures when you need protection. They're essential when you're at low health or have valuable creatures you want to protect.

Value Trades

Use Venomous creatures to kill large threats. A 2-mana Venomous creature killing an 8-mana creature is a huge value advantage!

Card Types

Creatures

Creatures are your main fighting force. Play them onto the board, and they can attack enemy creatures or the enemy hero. Creatures have Attack and Health stats.

  • Creatures cannot attack the turn they are played (they have "summoning sickness") unless they have Charge
  • When a creature attacks, both creatures deal damage to each other simultaneously
  • Creatures with 0 or less health are destroyed

Spells

Spells have an immediate effect when played and then are discarded. They can deal damage, heal, buff your creatures, draw cards, and more.

  • Some spells require a target - click on a valid target after playing
  • Spells resolve instantly and cannot be interrupted

Keywords

Keywords are special abilities that appear on cards. Understanding these is essential for strategic play.

Charge

Creatures with Charge can attack immediately when played, ignoring summoning sickness. This makes them excellent for surprise attacks or removing threats right away.

Example: Tyrannosaurus Rex (8 mana, 8/6 with Charge) - A devastating finisher that can deal 8 damage the turn it's played.

Guard

Creatures with Guard must be attacked before you can attack other targets. Your opponent cannot attack your hero or other creatures while you have a Guard creature on the board.

Example: Triceratops (5 mana, 3/7 with Guard) - A sturdy defender that protects your hero and weaker creatures.

Venomous

Creatures with Venomous instantly destroy any creature they damage, regardless of that creature's remaining health. Even 1 damage from a Venomous creature is fatal.

Example: Dilophosaurus (2 mana, 3/2 with Venomous) - Can trade with any creature regardless of size.

Armored X

Armored creatures reduce all incoming damage by X. This makes them much harder to kill and excellent at surviving combat. Armored 1 reduces damage by 1, Armored 2 reduces by 2, etc.

Example: Ankylosaurus (6 mana, 2/8 with Guard and Armored 2) - Takes 2 less damage from every attack, making its effective health even higher.

Extinct

Extinct is a death trigger - the effect activates when the creature dies. This can summon new creatures, deal damage, or trigger other powerful effects.

Example: Ancient Egg (1 mana, 0/3 with Extinct: Summon a 3/3 Hatchling) - A defensive card that provides value when destroyed.

Abilities

Battlecry

Battlecry effects trigger immediately when you play a card from your hand. They do not trigger when a creature is summoned by another effect.

  • Targeted Battlecry: Some battlecries require you to choose a target (click on a creature or hero)
  • Automatic Battlecry: Others affect all targets automatically (like "deal 2 damage to all enemies")

Example: Megalodon (7 mana, 7/5) - Battlecry: Destroy an enemy creature. When played, you can remove any enemy creature from the board.

Triggered Abilities

Some creatures have abilities that trigger during specific game events:

  • At the start of your turn: Triggers each time your turn begins
  • At the end of your turn: Triggers each time your turn ends
  • Whenever you draw a card: Triggers each time you draw
  • When this takes damage: Triggers when the creature is damaged
  • When you summon a creature: Triggers when any creature enters your board

Example: Oviraptor (2 mana, 2/2) - Whenever you draw a card, gain +1 Attack. Grows stronger with each card drawn.

Effect Types

Cards can have various effects:

  • Damage: Deal damage to creatures or heroes
  • Heal: Restore health to creatures or your hero
  • Buff (+X/+Y): Increase a creature's Attack and/or Health
  • Debuff (-X/-Y): Decrease a creature's Attack (cannot go below 0)
  • Draw: Draw cards from your deck
  • Destroy: Instantly destroy a creature regardless of health
  • Summon: Create new creatures on your board

Targeting Tip

When a card says "all enemies," it means all enemy creatures AND the enemy hero. "All enemy creatures" only affects creatures on the board. Pay attention to the exact wording!
